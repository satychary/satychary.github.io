<!-- ***************************************** --> # Text ## Our textbook is ['C++ For Everyone (2nd Ed)'](http://www.amazon.com/C-Everyone-Cay-S-Horstmann/dp/0470920920). You can either buy a used/new printed version, or buy/rent an electronic one. Do make sure you have access to the book, you'll need it. ---- #Sites (free, online material) ##C++ is a mature, widely-used language, which means there are numerous pages that are devoted to teaching it, discussing issues related to it, etc. Below is a small sampler. ###[LearnCpp.com](http://www.learncpp.com/) is an excellent site, with extremely well-written mini 'tutorials' on a huge number of C++ topics. ###[C++ FAQ](http://www.parashift.com/c++-faq/) - very thorough "go to" reference. ###[C++ reference](http://en.cppreference.com/w/cpp) - another good reference. ###[C++ Tutorial](http://www.cprogramming.com/tutorial/c++-tutorial.html) - a well-organized site, with detailed notes on every topic. ###[C++ in 7 pages](https://www.howtoforge.com/learning-c-cplusplus-step-by-step) : a fast-paced intro, uses lots of C (not C++) constructs. ###[Another C++ course](https://www.doc.ic.ac.uk/~wjk/C++Intro/CourseStructure.html) : clearly written, with plenty of program fragments and diagrams. ###A lengthy set of C++ topics: http://www.informit.com/guides/guide.aspx?g=cplusplus ###An online C++ course: http://cis.stvincent.edu/html/tutorials/swd/ <!-- ***************************************** -->
